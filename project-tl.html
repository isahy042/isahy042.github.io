I thoroughly enjoy exploring new tools and media, and making art through combining them. Thus, typhifolia land is a project where I can apply new tools to make art without constraint. My inspiration for the character design is somewhat based on the Classics of Mountains and Seas, also known as 山海经, a classical texts exploring 204 Chinese mythical figures.

This character’s inspiration came from the mythical creature 夫诸, a mischievous deer like creature that always brings flood to the places it goes. The illustration was done in procreate, while the animation was completed with Adobe After Effects and Premiere Pro. The 3D modeling was done in Nomad and Shapr3D. A version was originally colored in procreate, but this final version was selected over it as it employs textures that change based on the lighting around.

This 3D modeling is exhibited in Wellesley College’s “3, 2, 1” art show in the form of Interactive AR face tracking filter realized with RealityComposer. Photos from the exhibition will be attached below once available (by 12/10). In the future, I plan on creating content that are more immersive and interactive.
